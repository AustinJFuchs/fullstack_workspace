Alan Turing

The father of computer science

Alan Turing and his team at work at Bletchley Park.

Timeline of Dr. Turings' life:

  • 1912 - Born in London
  • 1934 - Graduated from King's College in Cambridge
  • 1935 - As his dissertation he proved the central limit theorem and was elected as a fellow of the King's
  • 1936 - Turing published his paper "On Computable Numbers"
  • 1939 - Started work at Bletchley Park, the day the United Kingdom declared war on Germany
  • 1952 - Convicted for "gross indecency" for homosexual acts
  • 1954 - dies of the age 41 due to cyanide poisoning. Believed to have been suicide.
